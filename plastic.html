Plastic Material (2020)

Virtual Installation

Mass Produce / Water Pollution / Useless / Capitalism　

Plastic Material is a virtual installation made with MAYA and body scan using Skanect and Kinect.　

This work is based on the issue of plastic pollution in the ocean that is popularly talked about among the younger generation right now. Taking advantage of plastic pollution in the ocean as the topic of modern common knowledge of everyone, this piece questions the society with AI and the identity of individuals in the system of capitalism and mass production.
I put a body scan and separated into body parts with textured with a package of brand logos that contribute to plastic pollution the most. In the small black room with mirrored walls, you can flow with plastic waste shaped body parts and think about the concept I talk about above.
The surface of the ocean is set as the level of your face, and that is something physical installation cannot put into practice. The idea of setting the level of the surface of the ocean as the viewer’s perspective was inspired by Up Against by Cheryl Pope and Eyes Wide Shut by Willy Verginer.
The four walls of the room are made out of mirrors so that the viewer can feel the ocean is infinite and they are a tiny existence there.

I was inspired by the game, "Detroit, Become Human." This game is created based on the world that Androids look identical to real humans and they work for humans as if they are slaves of humans. When Androids start to have an emotion, they are disposed of just like plastic waste. If technology advanced much more, and a similar society became possible, would humans be valuable? Even now, along with the society with automation taking over our jobs and there are so many people that have similar skills and background.

Would all individuals be valuable? How much are we different from the plastic waste flowing in the sea that cannot be distinguished individuals in the loop of being produced and wasted?
