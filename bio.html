I was born in 1998 in Osaka, Japan.
I spent 18 years of my life and moved to San Jose to study Art and English.
I am San Jose State University Digital Media Art BFA candidate in 2021.
I have two main inspiration of my work: researches of psychology
and abstract concepts we cannot see physically but exist in every culture.

Psychology has been my biggest interest more than digital media art.
The interest comes from one notice when I was a kid. "WHY AM I CRYING?"

A baby does not have much understanding in emotion.
Because they don't know the difference between sad, angry, jealous, and uncomfortable,
those emotion all come out as "crying" and that is why a baby cries a lot.
Even after I grew up to this age, I cannot bear crying
when I see someone get extremely mad or I get scolded by someone, especilly by mother.
I am not sad, not mad, not scared, not uncomfortable, and then why I cry?

Emotions are belong to us but I do not own them.
I often do not know why I am having a certain emotion or do not know what the emotion is.
I guess it is because my branches of emotion have not developed enough,
or I do not have enough vocabrary to describe this mind status?

Therefore, I use my art to explore the unknown emotions within us
and connection between them and the incidents that trigger them.

Exploring the abstract concepts we cannot see physically but exist in every culture
is a part of understanding unknow piece of myslef.
The examples would be "love" "god" "dream" "ghost" "lack" and "time."
Those words can be traslated into almost all languages.
I am interested in those because those concept needed regardless any culture.
Therefore, those are not cultural, geological, and racial.
This is purely about human, so I believe examining those would lead better understanding of human.
